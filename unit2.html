System of Equations and Inequalities

Linear Equations with Variables on Both Sides

Solving linear equations with variables on both sides entails manipulating terms on either side of the equation through addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division to isolate the variable, ultimately determining its value and satisfying the equality condition between the expressions on each side.

Compound Inequalities

Compound inequalities involve combining two or more inequalities with logical connectors such as "and" or "or" to describe a range of possible values for a variable, providing a more comprehensive understanding of the solution set and its relationship to the given conditions.

Linear Equations

Step by Step Approach

  1. Simplify each side separately.
  2. Combine like terms.
  3. Move variables to one side.
  4. Solve for the variable.
